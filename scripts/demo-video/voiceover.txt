[INTRO · 0:00–0:08 · over Sakura landing hero]

Sakura is a Solana-native execution-bounds verifier for AI agents. Where every other approach lets the out-of-bounds action land, then records or limits — Sakura reverts before the DeFi instruction executes.

[SHOT 1 · 0:08–0:35 · over IntentSigner UI]

A user signs one sentence in their wallet — for instance, "lend up to one thousand USDC into Kamino, ten thousand max per action, one week". Seven private values fold through a two-layer Poseidon hash into a thirty-two-byte commitment, anchored on Solana. The values stay in the browser. Only the hash reaches the chain.

[SHOT 2 · 0:35–1:30 · over terminal e2e flow]

Now the agent acts. The Sakura execute-with-intent-proof handler takes the user-signed commitment, a fresh Pyth price, a Switchboard cross-check, and a Groth16 proof generated in the browser. Six checks land atomically. The DeFi instruction either passes the gate with a valid proof — or the entire transaction reverts. Two hundred four thousand compute units, on-chain, via the alt-bn128 pairing syscall.

[SHOT 3 · 1:30–2:10 · over Solscan tx view]

The proof and the DeFi action share a single atomic version-zero transaction. They both land, or they both revert — inseparably. Every action writes a permanent ActionRecord PDA. Lawyers, auditors, counterparties — anyone can reconstruct the execution from public chain data alone. No off-chain monitor in the loop.

[SHOT 4 · 2:10–2:35 · over ActionHistory feed]

Inside the app, the same execution renders as a human-readable line — action type, target protocol, amount, oracle price, slot, and keccak-256 fingerprint. The user sees what their agent did. Always.
